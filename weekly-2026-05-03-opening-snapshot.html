GTM Bowtie — Primary Constraint
Right now we're running a marathon, but for every 4 steps forward we take 1 step backwards. The new business engine is strong — Win Rate is approaching top-quartile for B2B SaaS, and the team is closing deals at a healthy pace. But the retention side of the bowtie is eroding the ARR base faster than expansion can cover it. Every dollar won in new business is immediately worth less than a dollar, because a portion disappears through churn before it ever gets the chance to compound. Until NRR crosses 100%, the floor is falling as we build on top of it.

GTM Bowtie — Secondary Constraint
We're doing the hard part — generating qualified interest and getting buyers to commit time in their calendar — then losing them before the conversation even starts. Think of it as a restaurant with a serious no-show problem: the reservations are there, the table is set, and more than one in three guests simply don't walk through the door. The revenue that does show up converts well. The revenue that doesn't show up is the gap between where we are on quota and where we could have been.
