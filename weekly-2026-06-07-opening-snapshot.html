GTM Bowtie, Primary Constraint
On a net dollar basis the base is holding; trailing-12-month NRR of 106.1% means surviving customers spend more than they did a year ago, and total international ARR has grown to $1.10M. The problem hides underneath that number. Gross revenue retention is only 76.0%, so roughly a quarter of the book, about $206K annualized, churns or contracts every year and is being papered over by expansion and new logos. Picture a bucket that looks full only because the tap is running hard enough to mask a steady leak. More than a quarter of the customers active a year ago are gone today, an average customer life of just 3.8 years. Every point of gross-churn improvement drops almost directly to ARR and lifts LTV; this is the highest-value retention lever and it is invisible in the net number.

GTM Bowtie, Secondary Constraint
The new-business engine converts a smaller share of late-stage demand than it should. YTD we put 328 qualified opportunities into closing discussions and won 88, a 26.8% Win Rate that sits 8.2pp below the 35% target. Picture a closer who lands a quarter of his shots and never adjusts the range; the leads and demos feeding him are healthy, the shot conversion is not. Lifting Win Rate from 26.8% to 35% on the YTD pipeline run unlocks roughly $54,700 of additional ARR with no change in booking volume, enough to offset more than three-quarters of the CS-side leak quantified above. The cheapest ARR available right now is the deals already in the room.

Working Notes
Coverage window: 1 Jan 2026 → 9 Jun 2026 (today). MTD/YTD windows run to today, not to last week's end.
Intl-only scope: Companies with country = United States, USA, or Canada are excluded across all metrics. Residual contamination risk roughly 5 to 8% from ambiguous fields like "OR" or "CA".
Leads, MQLs, SQLs: From the lifecycle exports, deduplicated by Contact ID; leads dated by create date, MQL/SQL by lifecycle-stage entry date. These are international-wide top-of-funnel; roughly 42% of leads carry no Country/Region tag and Spain is a large source (expansion-market interest, not a managed market). Treat lead and MQL volumes as funnel context, broader than the four managed sales regions, not region-attributable pipeline.
Demos Booked, Demos Held, Show Rate: From ig-mom-show-rate, deduplicated by Custom Activity ID, keyed by 1:1 upcoming demo date.
Wins, Total, Win Rate: From ig-mom-win-rate, keyed by close date; Win Rate = Won ÷ (Won + Lost).
New ARR and Open Pipeline: From ig-mom-pipeline-volume, deduplicated by Deal ID. New ARR includes closed-won and upsell deals (105 closes). Open stages exclude won, upsell, and lost. Early = Demo Booked, Rebooked Demo, Demo Not Shown; Late = Demo Held. Note: Early is inflated by 90 "Demo Not Shown" no-show deals carrying $567,200; these are open but stalled and should be scrubbed for genuine viability before counting as coverage.
Avg Cycle: Mean Days to Close on YTD closed-won deals, from ig-mom-sales-cycle.
Churned and Expansion ARR: From int-l-cs-mom-churned-arr-by and int-l-cs-mom-expansion-arr, deal-level by Close Date, US/Canada excluded. CSAM mapping: Romania = Valentina Manta; DACH = Maria Mazur and Julia Seitz; UK/Italy = Piera Bellelli; ANZ = Paolo Zafra; Japan = Reina Kurosawa; all other markets = Other (no dedicated CSAM).
Net ARR Delta: Expansion ARR − Churned ARR. Expansion Coverage: Expansion ARR ÷ Churned ARR × 100.
NRR percentage: Not computed; requires a Starting ARR baseline not in the current data drop. Active Customers and Retained ARR are likewise unavailable.
