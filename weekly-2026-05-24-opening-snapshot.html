GTM Bowtie, Primary Constraint
The team is generating new ARR at a healthy clip but losing it faster on the other side of the bowtie. YTD we have closed $209,548 in new business, churned $104,414, and recovered only $35,612 through expansion; a net ARR delta of negative $68,801. Picture a leaky bucket where the tap is on full but a third of the water exits through holes in the bottom. The repair work isn't at the tap, it's at the bottom. For every $3 of revenue lost to churn, expansion claws back $1; the base is shrinking even before sales motion is considered. Until expansion at least matches churn, every quarter of growth carries forward a 33 percentage-point deadweight from the retention motion.

GTM Bowtie, Secondary Constraint
The new business engine is converting a smaller share of late-stage demand than it should. YTD we put 298 qualified opportunities into closing discussions and won 84 of them, a 28.2% Win Rate that sits 6.8pp below the 35% target. Think of it as a closer who shoots from beyond the arc, lands a quarter of his shots, and never adjusts the range. Each booked demo is currently worth $580 in expected ARR, versus the $720 it would yield at target Win Rate. Closing the Win Rate gap from 28.2% to 35% on the YTD pipeline run unlocks roughly $56,000 of additional ARR with no booking-volume change, restoring close to half of the CS-side leak quantified above.
