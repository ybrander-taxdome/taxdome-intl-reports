GTM Bowtie — Primary Constraint
The business is running a leaky bucket. The team closes 97 new deals and generates $186K in new ARR — and in the same period, $101K drains out through churn, offset by only $35K of expansion. For every 3 dollars the team earns in new ARR, roughly 1 dollar is already gone before it can compound. Think of it as filling a bathtub with the drain half open: you can keep pouring water in, but the waterline rises far slower than it should. Until expansion covers churn and net ARR movement turns positive, every new-business effort is partly treading water.

GTM Bowtie — Secondary Constraint
The team is booking demos, but 38% of them never happen. At a YTD show rate of 61.9%, for every 10 demos booked, 4 disappear before anyone speaks to the prospect. The conversion that does happen is solid — a 62.8% win rate per demo held YTD is strong. The problem is that the funnel loses nearly 4 in 10 opportunities before the sales conversation even starts. Think of it as a stage show where the theatre is sold out every night, but a third of the audience doesn't walk through the door. Closing the show rate gap from 62% to 80% would add roughly 68 more demos held per 373 booked — at current win rates, that's an additional 42 closes and ~$81K more ARR without any change to marketing volume.
