Executive Summary
State of International Growth

International sales in 2026 are tracking well on deal quality and ARR, with 74 deals won and $152K ARR YTD, already 48% of 2025's full-year ARR of $317K. Win rate at 22.2% is up from 19.5% in 2025, and blended ASP has risen to $2,067 vs. $1,618 last year, a 28% uplift. Romania leads on ARR contribution ($54K) driven by the highest ASP on the team at $3,338.

The show rate picture requires attention. Pipeline volume is up significantly. Demos booked have accelerated across all SDR-covered markets, but this growth has come at the cost of show rates. Comparing DACH to the other three markets points to a clear hypothesis: the combination of SDR activity (a new motion in 2026) and a significant uplift in marketing-generated pipeline volume has compressed show rates in Romania, UK and ANZ. DACH, running on inbound only with no SDR activity, serves as the control group here. Its 86% show rate reflects a pipeline that has not yet experienced that same volume and intent mix shift. This is not a CM performance issue. It is an expected growing pain of scaling two new pipeline channels simultaneously.

The key opportunity in Q2: UK has 133 demos booked YTD, by far the largest pipeline, but 52 deals lost and only 15 won, with $21K ARR. Improving qualification and show-up rates in the UK would have the largest single ARR impact of any action available to the team.

Sales cycle interpretation
Median days to close at 19.5 vs an average of 59 days indicates a bimodal close pattern. A large share of deals close very fast (likely inbound / high-intent) while a longer tail drags the average up. This pattern is consistent with 2025 (median 21 days, average 59 days), suggesting the underlying sales motion is stable. Focus should be on volume rather than cycle time.
Conclusion & Actions
Where to Focus in Q2
Priorities based on YTD data · owner to be assigned per action
01
UK pipeline quality audit
52 deals lost and 22% win rate with the team's largest pipeline. Work with SDR lead and marketing to tighten ICP targeting and improve booking quality. The highest single ARR lever available to the team.
02
Romania show rate: root cause
Show rate at 40.2% with March producing 33 lost deals alone. Investigate booking source quality, follow-up cadence and confirmation sequences. Deal quality is strong. The funnel leaks before the demo happens.
03
Study and spread the DACH model
DACH is the control group. Its 86% show rate on inbound-only pipeline, with no SDR volume dilution, helps isolate the impact of the new pipeline channels in other markets. Use this comparison to set a baseline for what good looks like and to design a qualification framework for SDR-booked and marketing-generated demos in Romania, UK and ANZ.
04
Scale ANZ top-of-funnel
ANZ is improving across every metric: win rate up, ASP up 58%, show rate solid. The constraint is now volume. Invest in SDR capacity or marketing activity to unlock the next tier of ARR.
05
Protect ASP discipline
Blended ASP is up 33% YTD. Romania ($3,424) and DACH ($2,162) are driving this. Monitor for discount pressure in UK (ASP down vs 2025) and reinforce value-based selling in all SDR handoffs.
06
April: partial data only
April data across all markets is thin and early. Show rates and win rates for April should not drive strategic conclusions. DACH, ANZ and Romania all show strong underlying trends when April is excluded.
