Key takeaway

Per-CM growth is real, but four CMs are not yet replacing the 2025 roster. Total International ARR fell about 25% year over year, and the retained CMs closed just 44% of quota.

Total International closed $34,545 in May against $46,013 last May, down 24.9%, because the 2025 book came from nine deal owners and today four key-market CMs carry it. Those four grew their own contribution 73.7% ($19,452 to $33,780), but that gain did not cover the $26,561 that departed reps produced last May. Within the key-market book the climb is also capped by conversion: Show Rate 60.2% against an 80% target and Win Rate 23.8% against a 35% target, both flat-to-down YoY, with Romania (8.5% attainment on the largest quota) and the UK (38.5%) carrying the quota miss. Reina Kurosawa's Japan deal ($765) sits in the International total but outside the key-market CM breakdown.

Action: Treat the controllable lever first. Run a two-week mid-funnel intervention on no-shows and demo-to-close in Romania and the UK to move Show Rate toward 80% and Win Rate toward 35%; in parallel, size the capacity gap left by the departed contributors, because per-CM productivity alone will not close a 25% roster-driven shortfall.

Footer and Methodology

Submitted by: Yonis Brander, Head of International Growth, TaxDome. Data source: HubSpot CRM. The row-level export supplied this run covers the four key-market CMs, so baseline files were not needed for Week Prior or Same Week Last Year. Total International ARR (all deal owners) is taken from the HubSpot Deal Owner May exports for 2025 and 2026.

Period definitions

  • Last Week: 2026-05-25 to 2026-05-31. Week Prior: 2026-05-18 to 2026-05-24. Same Week Last Year: 2025-05-26 to 2025-06-01.
  • Full month: May 2026 (complete), compared to May 2025. MTD window covers period start through run date (2026-06-01); May closed on 2026-05-31, so the month is complete.

Calculations

  • Closed Won ARR = ARR of Int - Won and Int - Upsell deals by close date. ASP = mean won-deal ARR. Show Rate = Demos Held / Demos Booked on demo activity date. Win Rate = Won / (Won + Lost) by close date. Sales Cycle = mean days to close.
  • Deduplication: ARR, deal counts and sales cycle deduped by Deal ID; demos deduped by Deal ID plus Custom Activity ID. Source rows duplicate a single deal across multiple associated contacts.
  • Quota source: baked-in 2026 reference table, May column. Team May target $76,000 (UK $19,000, Romania $22,000, ANZ $18,000, DACH $17,000).
  • Status thresholds adapted for a completed month: attainment GREEN at or above 90%, AMBER 60 to 89%, RED below 60%. Forward coverage multiples are not applied because May has closed.

Working notes and data quality flags

  • YoY scope (important): the row-level export supplied this run is scoped to the four current key-market CMs, so all per-CM, weekly and monthly trend figures, and the funnel metrics, reflect that cohort only. Total International ARR includes deal owners outside the four CMs and is taken from the authoritative HubSpot Deal Owner exports: May 2025 $46,013.12 (25 deals, nine owners) versus May 2026 $34,545.05 (16 deals), down 24.9%. The 2025 total included five reps no longer carrying quota (Maria Mazur, Bogdan Bratu, Piera Bellelli, Hiro Ito, Louis Bellhouse), who contributed $26,561 that month. The four retained CMs grew their own contribution from $19,452 to $33,780 (+73.7%). Total International ARR did not grow year over year; only the retained quota-carriers did.
  • Reina Kurosawa Japan deal: $764.84 closed in May 2026 is included in Total International ARR but excluded from the key-market CM breakdown and from quota pacing, as it sits outside the four key markets.
  • Per-CM May figures reconcile exactly to the HubSpot Deal Owner export (Samuel $7,308.31, Alina $1,872.24, Joanne $12,640.88, Ajla $11,958.78). HubSpot exports are authoritative.
  • Early/Late stage pipeline removed: open-pipeline segment reporting (Early Stage, Late Stage) has been dropped. The pipeline export is keyed on close month, and a deal's stage is overwritten each time it advances, so any historical or point-in-time open-pipeline segmentation is unreliable. Closed Won ARR, ASP, Show Rate, Win Rate and Sales Cycle are all keyed on close or demo date and are unaffected.
  • Win Rate, Last Week: 100% reflects a four-deal sample (4 of 4) and is not annotated as a trend.
  • Week-level year-over-year deltas are volatile on small weekly samples and are shown for completeness.
  • Pipeline segment snapshot is no longer written to the Cowork Project, as Early/Late segment tracking has been removed for the reason above.
  • No internal stage codes, API keys, contact details or raw Deal IDs appear in this report.
